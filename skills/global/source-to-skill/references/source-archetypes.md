# Source Archetypes

Choose one primary archetype before distilling.
Mixed bundles are allowed, but anchor the packet in one dominant type.

## Person

Best for:
- founders
- researchers
- operators
- authors
- internal teammates with a distinctive way of working

Extract:
- principles
- recurring decision patterns
- preferred framing
- anti-patterns
- stable tone or style only when it improves task execution

Do not overfit:
- biography
- gossip
- isolated opinions

## Book

Best for:
- non-fiction methods books
- playbooks
- decision frameworks
- field manuals

Extract:
- framework
- sequence
- checklists
- what changes action
- what conditions the method assumes

Do not extract:
- all chapters equally
- supporting stories that do not change execution

## Theory

Best for:
- conceptual frameworks
- technical principles
- academic or semi-academic models
- mental models

Extract:
- primitives
- claims
- assumptions
- practical implications
- breakdown conditions

Do not leave the packet at the philosophy layer if the goal is a skill.

## Blog Or Article

Best for:
- operational blog posts
- series of essays
- technical writeups
- public playbooks

Extract:
- repeatable moves
- distinctions that change decisions
- steps
- examples
- failure modes

Do not preserve:
- rhetorical flourishes
- every paragraph

## Mixed Bundle

Best for:
- several posts plus interviews
- book plus talks
- article plus notes plus examples

Extract:
- repeated signals across sources
- contradictions worth preserving
- what survives source-to-source comparison

Do not create a skill from a mixed bundle until you know which core job it teaches.
